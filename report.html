Customer Segmentation Report (K=4)

1. Business Problem

Problem: Segment customers based on behavior and characteristics

Goal:

  • Targeted marketing
  • Pricing strategies
  • Product design
  • Retention campaigns

2. Key Methodology

  • K-Means clustering applied
  • Features standardized (StandardScaler)
  • Elbow + Silhouette used for evaluation
  • K fixed at 4 based on business + interpretability

3. Important Modeling Decisions

  • Savings balance removed → highly correlated with investment balance
  • Age excluded → used only for profiling
  • Feature selection focused on behavior + financial activity

4. Segment Overview (K=4)

S0 · Conservative Wealth Holders

High assets, low risk tolerance, long tenure

S1 · High-risk Growth Investors

High investment balance, high risk tolerance

S2 · Loyal Low-income Users

Long tenure, high loyalty, lower income

S3 · Power Transactors

Highest activity, frequent logins and transactions

5. Cluster Interpretation Summary

  • S0 → Stable but low-growth customers
  • S1 → High-value growth-focused segment
  • S2 → Loyal but financially constrained
  • S3 → Most active and monetizable users

6. Business Recommendations (Behavior-Based)

ML / Programmatic
S0 customers show stable behavior and low risk preference, so models should prioritize wealth preservation signals rather than aggressive growth targeting.

S1 customers have high investment activity, making them ideal for premium targeting and lookalike modeling.

S2 users are loyal but lower income, so models should focus on engagement and retention uplift rather than monetization.

S3 users show the strongest behavioral signals, making them the best candidates for high-value targeting models.
Offline / CRM
S0 users show moderate engagement, so email-based communication is more effective than push notifications.

S1 users actively manage investments, so educational and premium content campaigns are effective.

S2 users are loyal but not highly active digitally, so push + SMS reminders help maintain engagement.

S3 users frequently log in, so in-app messaging and real-time engagement are most effective.
Pricing
S0 users prefer stability → low-fee, safe products.

S1 users show high willingness to pay → premium pricing possible.

S2 users are price-sensitive → cashback / free-tier strategies required.

S3 users generate high volume → subscription or transaction-based pricing optimal.
Retention
S0 churn signal: drop in activity → use financial safety reassurance.

S1 churn signal: drop in investment → proactive alerts needed.

S2 churn risk is low but engagement drop should be monitored via push.

S3 churn signal: decrease in login or transaction frequency → immediate intervention needed.

7. Key Insight

Although clustering separation is not extremely strong, the segmentation provides meaningful operational groups. Behavior-based differentiation (activity, risk, financial status) enables practical marketing and retention strategies.

8. Conclusion

  • K-Means successfully applied
  • K=4 selected for interpretability
  • Segments are actionable for business use
  • Behavior-driven strategy is key outcome
