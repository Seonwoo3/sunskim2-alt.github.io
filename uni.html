Why Larger K Worked Better

  • Small K values were unstable and more affected by local noise.
  • As K increased, the model became less sensitive to outliers and produced more stable predictions.
  • This dataset appears to reward broader neighborhood voting rather than highly local decisions.
Important caveat: KNN achieved the best F1-score and very high recall, but its precision remains lower than the tree. This means it catches more positives, but may also generate more false alarms.

5. Business Cost Interpretation

Why KNN Can Be Valuable

  • High recall means fewer missed positive cases.
  • If missing a true renewal-risk or intervention-relevant customer is costly, KNN is attractive.
  • The model appears effective at detecting broader customer similarity patterns.

Why Tree Still Matters

  • Higher precision means fewer unnecessary positive predictions.
  • If operational resources are limited, better precision can reduce wasted outreach.
  • Its rules are directly usable for management communication and customer success playbooks.
Cost framing: High recall reduces false negatives, but lower precision can increase false positives. In practice, the better model depends on whether the business is more concerned with missing real opportunities or overspending resources on low-risk accounts.

6. Executive Recommendation

Recommended Operating Model

  • Primary scoring model: KNN with K = 29
  • Primary explanation model: Classification Tree
  • Reason: KNN currently provides the best out-of-sample F1-score and strongest recall, while the tree provides the clearest interpretation of drivers and decision rules.
  • Practical recommendation: Use KNN for predictive scoring and use the tree to explain drivers, support executive discussion, and design retention actions.
Final management message: If the priority is predictive power, select KNN(k=29). If the priority is transparent explanation, use the tree as the supporting interpretation layer. The strongest combined strategy is to use both models together for different purposes.
