KNN Interpretation

5. Business Interpretation: Recall vs. F1-Score

Why Recall Matters

  • Recall becomes important when the company wants to identify as many at-risk customers as possible.
  • A False Negative means a true non-renewal customer is missed.
  • That can directly lead to lost revenue because the company fails to intervene in time.

Why F1-Score Matters

  • F1-Score balances Precision and Recall.
  • A model with high Recall but weak Precision may create too many false alarms.
  • That leads to resource waste because customer success teams spend time on accounts that were not actually at risk.
Executive interpretation: For intervention strategy, Recall is strategically important. For final model selection, however, F1-Score is the more balanced summary metric because it captures both missed-risk cost and wasted-resource cost.

6. Final Recommendation

Recommended Model: Decision Tree

  • Performance: Decision Tree delivered the strongest overall metrics across Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and F1-Score.
  • Interpretability: Its rule-based structure is easier to explain to management and operational teams.
  • Business usability: The model is better suited for customer segmentation, intervention prioritization, and executive reporting.
  • Role of KNN: KNN remains useful as a benchmark model, but it is less suitable as the primary decision-support tool in this business context.
